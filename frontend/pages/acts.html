Acts Covered under the New Criminal Laws

Understanding India's New Criminal Justice Framework. A comprehensive transition guide from the colonial-era penal codes to the modern Bharatiya Sanhitas.

Quick Reference Framework

Substantive Law Act No. 45 of 2023

Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS)

Replaces: Indian Penal Code (IPC), 1860
Effective: July 1, 2024

The BNS consolidates and amends the provisions relating to offenses and for matters connected therewith. It introduces community service as a form of punishment, addresses new age crimes, and modernizes the definitions to suit contemporary societal needs while repealing the colonial-era IPC.

Procedural Law Act No. 46 of 2023

Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita (BNSS)

Replaces: Code of Criminal Procedure (CrPC), 1973
Effective: July 1, 2024

BNSS outlines the procedure for investigation, arrest, and trial. It places a heavy emphasis on the use of digital evidence, forensic sciences in investigations, and establishes strict timelines for various stages of legal proceedings to expedite justice delivery.

Evidentiary Law Act No. 47 of 2023

Bharatiya Sakshya Adhiniyam (BSA)

Replaces: Indian Evidence Act, 1872
Effective: July 1, 2024

The BSA modernizes the rules regarding admissibility of evidence in legal proceedings. A key change is the formal recognition of electronic and digital records as primary evidence, placing them on par with traditional paper documents.
